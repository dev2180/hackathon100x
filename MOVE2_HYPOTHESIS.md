# Move 2 — Hypothesis

**Scope:** Mid-build beginners with multi-stage domain AI products. Excludes: no-idea beginner, fear-of-shipping beginner, motivation-only beginner.

## The Claim

This person isn't blocked by missing knowledge. They're blocked because they hold every build stage at equal weight — setup, agents, iteration, custom LLM all feel equally urgent and equally hard — and they can't see the time/cost/scale tradeoff inside each stage. Flat terrain, no levers visible. So they freeze. Handed one sentence that names the flattening (not their ignorance), they recognize it sharply and act — pick a first step, shelve the far-off hard stage, or ask unprompted what's actually first.

## Discriminating Prediction

Their instinctive fix will be one of two wrong moves — go learn the hardest stage first, or "break it into steps and have someone do it for me." The sentence predicts both fixes keep them stuck: the first freezes them longer, the second turns them into a buyer of their own product instead of its builder.

## Result That Proves Right

≥1 of 2 subjects goes quiet / "how'd you know," AND takes a concrete smaller action: picks stage 1, drops the LLM-training rabbit hole, or asks unprompted "so what do I build first."

## Result That Means Horoscope

Subjects nod warmly, "yeah that's me / I'm overwhelmed," and change nothing.

## The Bet

I'm betting at least 1 of my 2 subjects reacts to the sentence — not "yeah that's me" nodding, but actually doing something: picks one stage to start, drops the custom-LLM rabbit hole, or asks me unprompted "okay so what do I build first."

If both of them just nod and walk away unchanged — I lost the bet. That means I wrote a horoscope, not a diagnosis. I'll say so straight in the report: "0 for 2, my sentence didn't predict anything, it just sounded nice."

## Why Narrow Scope

A sentence true of every stuck beginner is the horoscope. Scoping to the mid-build beginner makes the sentence false of most others — which is the literal scoring bar.
